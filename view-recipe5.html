INDIAN SAMOSA RECIPE

Ingredients

  • 2 cups All-purpose flour (maida)
  • 4 tablespoons Oil or Ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 teaspoon Carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • 3-4 Medium-sized Potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup Green peas (optional, boiled)
  • 1-2 Green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon Ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon Coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon Red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dry mango powder (amchur) or 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons Oil for cooking the filling
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

    1.Mix the Ingredients:
  1. In a large bowl, add all-purpose flour, carom seeds, salt, and oil or ghee.
  2. Rub the flour and oil mixture with your fingers until it resembles bread crumbs. This helps create a flaky crust.
    3. 2.Knead the Dough:
  3. Gradually add water to the flour mixture and knead into a firm dough.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30-40 minutes.
    5. 3.Cook the Spices:
  5. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
  6. Add chopped green chilies and grated ginger. Sauté for a minute.
    7. 4.Add Mashed Potatoes:
  7. Add the mashed potatoes and boiled green peas to the pan. Stir well.
    8. 5.Add Spices:
  8. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, amchur (or lemon juice), and salt.
  9. Mix everything well and cook for 3-4 minutes on low heat.
    10. 6.Finish the Filling:
  10. Turn off the heat and add chopped coriander leaves. Let the filling cool completely before using it to fill the samosas.
    11. 7.Roll the Dough:
  11. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls. Roll each ball into a thin oval or round shape (about 6 inches in diameter).
    12. 8.Cut and Shape:
  12. Cut the rolled dough in half to form two semi-circles.
  13. Take one semi-circle, and form a cone shape by bringing the straight edge together and sealing it with water.
    14. 9.Fill the Cone:
  14. Stuff the prepared cone with the potato filling.
  15. Seal the open edge by pressing and folding it to form a triangular samosa shape.
    16. 10.Heat Oil:
  16. Heat oil in a deep pan or wok on medium heat. Make sure the oil is not too hot; samosas should fry slowly to become crispy.
    17. 11.Fry
  17. Gently slide the samosas into the hot oil and fry them on medium-low heat.
  18. Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, flipping them occasionally.
    19. 12.Drain Excess Oil:
  19. Remove the samosas from the oil and place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
